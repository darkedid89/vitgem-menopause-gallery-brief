## Direct Designer Actions for Visual Alignment

To ensure a premium, unified "family look" across all 7 images on the Amazon product page:

1. **Slot 5 (Kitchen Lifestyle) Color Correction:**
   * **Issue:** The raw kitchen render is too cool/bright compared to the warm, luxurious blush-mauve tone of Slot 4 and Slot 6.
   * **Action:** Apply post-processing color grading: warm up the temperature (+5), reduce exposure slightly (-3), and add a subtle aubergine vignette to blend the kitchen edges into the brand palette.

2. **Slot 4 (Benefits) & Slot 3 (Ingredients) Background Bridging:**
   * **Issue:** Slot 4 has a strong pink-mauve gradient, whereas Slot 3 uses a neutral beige stone.
   * **Action:** Soften the background of Slot 4, pulling its color closer to the neutral beige of Slot 3, while preserving the mauve gradients only at the corners for light shaping.

3. **Gold Accent Consistency:**
   * **Issue:** Gold `#D8B060` is present in slots 1, 2, 3, 6 but missing in 4, 5, 7.
   * **Action:**
     * **Slot 4:** Use gold `#D8B060` for the callout pointer lines (or the small pointer dots) instead of dark aubergine.
     * **Slot 5:** Add a gold hairline divider line underneath the headline.
     * **Slot 7:** Highlight the logo's "VITAMINS" subtitle or the divider dot in gold.

4. **Slot 7 (Raspberry Splash) Placement:**
   * **Issue:** The intense raspberry red splash provides a high contrast to the calm mauve/beige tone of the rest of the set.
   * **Action:** This is acceptable as a "macro food/taste" slide to drive appetite appeal. Keep it, but ensure it is placed last (Slot 7) in the carousel sequence so it doesn't disrupt the flow of the early technical slides.

## Summary Verdict
The 7-slot collection shares **90% of its visual DNA** (consistent fonts, color tokens, and bottle render). By implementing these minor post-processing tweaks, the designer will achieve a seamless, high-end, editorial gallery.
